Continuous Denoising Enables One-step Language Modeling

Abstract

We introduce Flow-based Language Models (FLM), a method that enables one-step parallel text generation through continuous denoising, achieving an 8.3× speedup over existing approaches.

Unlike autoregressive models that generate tokens sequentially, FLM leverages flow matching to directly map noise to coherent text in a single step. Our key insight is that continuous denoising in the learned embedding space enables more effective flow-based generation compared to discrete diffusion approaches. We introduce flow map distillation (FLM-distill), which distills multi-step flow models into efficient one-step generators while preserving generation quality.

We demonstrate that FLM achieves competitive performance with state-of-the-art diffusion language models while requiring significantly fewer inference steps. Our one-step FLM-distill model matches the quality of 8-step discrete diffusion models, enabling practical deployment of non-autoregressive language generation. We evaluate FLM on multiple text generation benchmarks, showing that continuous denoising consistently outperforms discrete approaches in both quality and efficiency.
